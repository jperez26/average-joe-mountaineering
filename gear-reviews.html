Gear Reviews

Equipment I've tested in the mountains and trust with my life

These are honest reviews of gear I've actually used on climbs. I've included pros, cons, and my genuine recommendations based on real-world mountain experience.

Arc'teryx Alpha FL Jacket

★★★★★ 5/5 Stars

Category: Hard Shell Jacket

Price: ~$450 USD

Weight: 290g (M)

Used On: Mt. Hood, Mt. Washington

Pros:

  • Best-in-class weather protection - truly waterproof in extreme conditions
  • Incredibly durable Gore-Tex fabric resists tearing
  • Superior cut and design for alpine climbing - not restrictive
  • Strategic reinforcement in high-wear areas
  • Excellent venting options for temperature regulation

Cons:

  • Premium price point may be challenging for budget climbers
  • Slightly restrictive when wearing heavy packs
  • Requires regular seam sealing maintenance

Verdict:

This is the best hard shell I've ever owned. Used on both Hood and Washington in severe weather, it performed flawlessly. The premium price is justified for serious mountaineers. This jacket will last decades with proper care.

Black Diamond Megamid Tent

★★★★☆ 4/5 Stars

Category: Alpine Tent

Price: ~$450 USD

Weight: 1.8 kg (4 lbs)

Used On: Mt. Hood, Mt. Washington

Pros:

  • Incredibly lightweight for a 4-season tent
  • Exceptional wind resistance - apex design is brilliant
  • Easy setup in difficult terrain with minimal footprint
  • Superior ventilation prevents condensation buildup
  • Quality construction from Black Diamond

Cons:

  • Interior space is minimal - more shelter than livable space
  • Setup requires perfect conditions or stakes won't hold
  • Expensive for the livable area offered
  • Single wall construction means condensation is inevitable

Verdict:

Perfect for climbers who prioritize weight and wind resistance over space. I use this for alpine mountaineering above 10,000 feet. Not ideal for extended high-altitude camps, but brilliant for 2-3 night push camps on technical peaks.

Petzl Nomad Crampons

★★★★★ 5/5 Stars

Category: Technical Crampons

Price: ~$200 USD

Weight: 625g per pair

Used On: Mt. Hood (extensive glacier and ice)

Pros:

  • Superior front points for steep ice climbing
  • Excellent compatibility with mountaineering boots
  • Anti-balling plates prevent ice accumulation
  • Easy to adjust even with gloves on
  • Proven durability in extreme conditions

Cons:

  • Heavier than some lightweight alternatives
  • Takes time to master proper fitting
  • Requires maintenance and sharpening

Verdict:

These are the gold standard for alpine mountaineering. On Hood, they performed perfectly on everything from gentle glacier traverses to steep ice climbing. Worth every penny. Buy once, use for decades.

La Sportiva Neproma GTX Boots

★★★★☆ 4/5 Stars

Category: Alpine Mountaineering Boots

Price: ~$350 USD

Weight: ~800g per boot

Used On: Mt. Hood, Mt. Washington

Pros:

  • Excellent insulation for extreme cold
  • Gore-Tex lining keeps feet dry in wet conditions
  • Compatible with crampons and microspikes
  • Aggressive tread pattern for complex terrain
  • Relatively comfortable for technical peaks

Cons:

  • Takes considerable break-in period
  • Heavy boots contribute to leg fatigue
  • Fit can be inconsistent between sizes
  • Expensive maintenance and replacement soles

Verdict:

Solid choice for alpine mountaineering below 15,000 feet. They kept my feet warm and dry on both Hood and Washington. Break them in thoroughly before your expedition. Budget extra time for training climbs in these boots.

Black Diamond Ice Tools Combo

★★★★★ 5/5 Stars

Category: Ice Axes

Price: ~$400 (pair) USD

Weight: ~720g per tool

Used On: Mt. Hood

Pros:

  • Versatile design handles both climbing and self-belay
  • Excellent pick and adze geometry for alpine ice
  • Comfortable grip even with thick gloves
  • Durable construction from premium materials
  • Well-balanced for extended use

Cons:

  • Not specialized for pure ice climbing (but not needed for Alpine work)
  • Requires proper training to use safely
  • Expensive initial investment

Verdict:

Perfect for alpine mountaineers. These tools made climbing Hood's glaciers secure and efficient. The self-arrest capability is essential. Invest in proper training before purchasing.

Osprey Kestrel 48 Backpack

★★★★☆ 4/5 Stars

Category: Alpine Backpack

Price: ~$230 USD

Weight: 1.86 kg (4.1 lbs) empty

Used On: Mt. Hood, Mt. Washington

Pros:

  • Comfortable load distribution even with heavy loads
  • Modular design allows customization
  • Excellent weather resistance
  • Multiple attachment points for gear
  • Great lifetime customer service from Osprey

Cons:

  • Slightly heavier than some dedicated alpine packs
  • Can be overkill for day climbs
  • Packed size is bulky

Verdict:

Excellent all-around alpine pack. Carried it on both Hood and Washington with heavy loads. The comfort and organization features justify the weight. This pack will last decades with Osprey's warranty.
