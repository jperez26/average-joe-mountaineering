Future Expeditions

Peaks I'm training for and expeditions on the horizon

These mountains represent my next challenges. Each requires specific training, preparation, and careful planning. I'm committed to summiting them safely and responsibly.

2026

Mt. Shasta

14,179 ft Cascade Range, California Summer 2026

Overview

California's second-highest and most isolated peak. Mt. Shasta is a massive stratovolcano with multiple glaciers and technical terrain. The mountain offers a perfect intermediate step between Hood and higher altitude objectives.

Climbing Approach

Route: Clear Creek Route via Hotlum-Bolam Ridge | Difficulty: Difficult (Grade II-III) | Technical Rating: Class 4 rock, ice climbing

Preparation Focus

  • Higher altitude acclimatization protocols
  • Advanced ice climbing technique refinement
  • Endurance training for longer summit day
  • Multi-day expedition logistics

Timeline

Training: March-May 2026 | Expedition: June 2026

2026

Mt. Baker

10,781 ft North Cascades, Washington Late Summer 2026

Overview

The northernmost major volcano in the Cascade Range. Baker offers excellent mountaineering with extensive glacier systems and relatively straightforward climbing. A great technical climbing objective with stunning views.

Climbing Approach

Route: Easton Glacier Route | Difficulty: Moderate-Difficult (Grade II) | Technical Rating: Ice climbing, crevasse rescue practice

Preparation Focus

  • Glacier travel and crevasse rescue drills
  • Mixed terrain climbing (rock and ice)
  • Self-rescue and partner assistance techniques
  • Rapid acclimatization training

Timeline

Training: June-August 2026 | Expedition: August 2026

2026

Mt. Rainier

14,411 ft Cascade Range, Washington Late Summer 2026

Overview

The highest peak in the Cascade Range and a serious high-altitude mountaineering objective. Rainier combines altitude, glacier climbing, technical terrain, and unpredictable weather. One of the most challenging and rewarding peaks in the continental US.

Climbing Approach

Route: Disappointment Cleaver Route | Difficulty: Difficult (Grade III-IV) | Technical Rating: High altitude, extensive glaciation, technical rock

Preparation Focus

  • Serious altitude acclimatization (14,000+ ft)
  • Advanced glacier and crevasse techniques
  • Weather decision-making at altitude
  • Sleep systems for extreme cold
  • Multi-day expedition management

Timeline

Training: July-September 2026 | Expedition: September 2026

2027

Bolivian High Altitude Expedition

19,000+ ft Andes Mountains, Bolivia Early 2027

Overview

My first serious high-altitude expedition to the Bolivian Andes. This expedition will tackle two major peaks, introducing me to true high-altitude mountaineering above 19,000 feet. This experience will be critical preparation for bigger objectives.

Target Peaks

  • Huayna Potosí (6,088 m / 19,974 ft): A technical alpine climb requiring ice climbing and crevasse rescue skills. Perfect introduction to high altitude.
  • Illimani (6,438 m / 21,122 ft): Bolivia's highest peak and more serious high-altitude objective combining altitude with technical terrain.

Preparation Focus

  • Systematic altitude acclimatization protocols
  • Altitude sickness recognition and prevention
  • Extended cold weather camping
  • International expedition logistics
  • High-altitude decision making and risk management

Timeline

Intensive Training: October 2026-December 2026 | Expedition: January-February 2027
