Mt. Shasta

14,179 ft Cascade Range, California Summer 2026

Overview

California's second-highest and most isolated peak. Mt. Shasta is a massive stratovolcano and it offers my first chance to spend the night at elevation in a bivvy. The mountain offers a perfect intermediate step between Hood and higher altitude objectives.

Climbing Approach

Route: | Difficulty: Difficult (Grade II-III) | Technical Rating: Class 4 rock, ice climbing

Preparation Focus

  • Higher altitude acclimatization protocols
  • A sleep system that can be used on even higher peaks
  • Endurance training for longer summit day
  • Multi-day trip logistics

Timeline

Training: April-June 2026 | Expedition: July 2026

2026

Mt. Baker

10,781 ft North Cascades, Washington Late Summer/Fall 2026

Overview

The northernmost major volcano in the Cascade Range. Baker offers excellent mountaineering with extensive glacier systems and relatively straightforward climbing. A great technical climbing objective with stunning views.

Climbing Approach

Route: Easton Glacier Route | Difficulty: Moderate-Difficult (Grade II) | Technical Rating: Ice climbing, crevasse rescue practice

Preparation Focus

  • Glacier travel and crevasse rescue drills
  • Mixed terrain climbing (rock and ice)
  • Self-rescue and partner assistance techniques
  • Rapid acclimatization training

Timeline

Training: July-August 2026 | Expedition: September 2026

Mt. Rainier

14,411 ft Cascade Range, Washington March 2027

Overview

The highest peak in the Cascade Range and a serious high-altitude mountaineering objective. Rainier combines altitude, glacier climbing, technical terrain, and unpredictable weather. One of the most challenging and rewarding peaks in the continental US.

Climbing Approach

Route: Disappointment Cleaver Route although one day I would like to do Liberty Ridge| Difficulty: Difficult (Grade III-IV) | Technical Rating: High altitude, extensive glaciation, technical rock

Preparation Focus

  • Having Fun
  • Advanced glacier and crevasse techniques
  • Weather decision-making at altitude
  • Sleep systems for extreme cold
  • Multi-day fast hiking

Timeline

Training: September 2026 | Expedition: March 2027

2027

Bolivian High Altitude Triple Crown Expedition

20,000+ ft Andes Mountains, Bolivia Early 2027

Overview

My first serious high-altitude expedition to the Bolivian Andes. This expedition will tackle three major peaks, introducing me to true high-altitude mountaineering above 19,000 feet. This experience will be critical preparation for bigger objectives.

Target Peaks

  • Huayna Potosí (6,088 m / 19,974 ft): A technical alpine climb requiring ice climbing and crevasse rescue skills. Perfect introduction to high altitude.
  • Illimani (6,438 m / 21,122 ft): Bolivia's highest peak and more serious high-altitude objective combining altitude with technical terrain.
  • Pequeño Alpamayo (5,410 m / 17,749 ft): stunningly aesthetic peak in the Condoriri group, famous for its iconic "knife-edge" summit ridge and technical alpine character.

Preparation Focus

  • Systematic altitude acclimatization protocols
  • Altitude sickness recognition and prevention
  • Extended cold weather camping
  • International expedition logistics
  • High-altitude decision making and risk management

Timeline

Intensive Training: October 2026-December 2026 | Expedition: Sometime 2027
