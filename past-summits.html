Past Summits

Mountains I've conquered and lessons learned along the way

Mt. Washington

6,288 ft White Mountains, New Hampshire Summer 2024

The Challenge

Mt. Washington is the highest peak in the Northeast and home to some of the most extreme weather in North America. Known for rapid weather changes and harsh conditions, this peak teaches humility and respect for mountain forces.

Route Details

  • Route: Tuckerman Ravine Trail
  • Ascent Time: 4-5 hours
  • Descent Time: 3-4 hours
  • Key Features: Alpine zone, exposed ridges, technical scrambling
  • Difficulty: Moderate-Difficult

Key Takeaways

This climb taught me the importance of weather awareness and starting early. The exposed alpine zone leaves no room for error, and the rapidly changing conditions demand respect. I learned that physical fitness alone isn't enough—mental preparation and decision-making are equally critical.

Mt. Hood

11,240 ft Cascade Range, Oregon Summer 2025

The Challenge

Mt. Hood is a serious alpine objective combining technical rock climbing, ice climbing, and crevasse navigation. As an active stratovolcano, it presents unique hazards and requires solid mountaineering fundamentals.

Route Details

  • Route: South Side via Palmer Glacier
  • Total Distance: ~6 miles roundtrip
  • Ascent Time: 6-7 hours
  • Key Features: Glaciers, crevasses, technical scrambling, ice climbing
  • Difficulty: Difficult (Grade II)

Key Takeaways

Hood was my graduation to serious mountaineering. The combination of altitude, ice climbing, and crevasse rescue scenarios made this a transformative experience. I learned the critical importance of rope team coordination, practiced crevasse rescue in real conditions, and gained confidence in my technical abilities. This summit prepared me for higher, more remote peaks.
